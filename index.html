The Web

The Web (or World Wide Web) is a collection of documents and other files. Most of the documents are made using Hypertext Markup Language (HTML).

What is HTML?

HTML stands for Hypertext Markup Language. It's used to define the structure or layout of a web page.

<!DOCTYPE html>
<html>
  <head>
    <title>Page Title</title>
  </head>
  <body>
    <h1>My First Heading</h1>
    <p>My first paragraph.</p>
  </body>
</html>

About Ethiopia

Ethiopia, officially known as the Federal Democratic Republic of Ethiopia, is a country located in the Horn of Africa. It is the second-most populous nation in Africa, with over 120 million people, and has one of the longest histories of any country in the world.

Flag of Ethiopia

Ethiopian History

Ethiopia is one of the oldest countries in the world, with a history dating back thousands of years. It is the only African country that was never formally colonized, although it was occupied by Italy from 1936 to 1941.

Key historical points:

  • Ancient Kingdom of Aksum (c. 100 AD - 940 AD) - One of the great powers of the ancient world
  • Adoption of Christianity - In the 4th century, making Ethiopia one of the first Christian nations
  • Zagwe Dynasty (c. 900-1270) - Known for building rock-hewn churches in Lalibela
  • Solomonic Dynasty (1270-1974) - Claimed descent from King Solomon and the Queen of Sheba
  • Italian Occupation (1936-1941) - Part of Italian East Africa
  • Derg Regime (1974-1991) - Marxist-Leninist military dictatorship
  • Federal Democratic Republic (1991-present) - Current government structure

Geography

Ethiopia has diverse geography ranging from high mountains to deep valleys. The Ethiopian Highlands cover most of the country and are divided by the Great Rift Valley.

Notable geographical features:

  • Simien Mountains - UNESCO World Heritage Site with unique wildlife
  • Danakil Depression - One of the hottest places on Earth
  • Lake Tana - Source of the Blue Nile River
  • Blue Nile Falls - Known as "Tis Issat" or "Smoking Water"
  • Bale Mountains - Home to the Ethiopian wolf

Economy

Ethiopia has one of the fastest-growing economies in Africa, with an average annual GDP growth of around 10% over the past decade.

Key economic sectors:

  • Agriculture - Coffee is Ethiopia's largest export
  • Manufacturing - Textiles, leather goods, and processed foods
  • Services - Growing telecommunications and banking sectors
  • Construction - Major infrastructure projects including dams and railways

Despite rapid growth, Ethiopia remains one of the poorest countries in the world, with significant challenges in poverty reduction and economic diversification.

Population & Regions

Ethiopia has a population of over 120 million people, making it the second most populous country in Africa after Nigeria.

The country is divided into 11 regional states:

  1. Tigray
  2. Afar
  3. Amhara
  4. Oromia
  5. Somali
  6. Benishangul-Gumuz
  7. Southern Nations, Nationalities, and Peoples' Region (SNNPR)
  8. Gambela
  9. Harari
  10. Sidama
  11. South West Ethiopia Peoples' Region

Additionally, there are two chartered cities: Addis Ababa (the capital) and Dire Dawa.

Culture, Religion & Ethnic Groups

Ethnic Groups

Ethiopia is incredibly diverse with over 80 ethnic groups. The largest include:

  • Oromo - 34.5% of the population
  • Amhara - 26.9%
  • Somali - 6.2%
  • Tigray - 6.1%
  • Sidama - 4%
  • Gurage - 2.5%
  • Welayta - 2.3%

Religion

Ethiopia has a diverse religious landscape:

  • Christianity - 67.3% (Ethiopian Orthodox 43.8%, Protestant 22.8%, Catholic 0.7%)
  • Islam - 31.3%
  • Traditional faiths - 0.6%
  • Other/None - 0.8%

Language

Amharic is the official working language of the federal government, but there are over 90 individual languages spoken in Ethiopia. English is the most widely spoken foreign language and is taught in schools.
