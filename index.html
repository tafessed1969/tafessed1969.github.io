About Ethiopia

History

Ethiopia is one of the world's oldest nations. It includes ancient kingdoms such as D’mt and Aksum, and it successfully resisted colonization — notably winning the Battle of Adwa (1896).

Geography

Located in the Horn of Africa, Ethiopia features highlands, the Great Rift Valley, the Blue Nile (Abay), and varied climates from cool mountains to hot lowlands.

Economy

The economy relies on agriculture (coffee, teff, pulses), growing manufacturing and services, and energy projects (hydropower). Ethiopia has seen strong growth in recent decades.

Population & Regions

Population is over 100 million. Major administrative regions include Amhara, Oromia, Tigray, Somali, Afar, South (SNNPR), Sidama, Benishangul-Gumuz, Gambela, Harari, Southwest, and Addis Ababa city.

Religion

Major religions: Ethiopian Orthodox Christianity, Islam, Protestant Christianity — with ancient monastic traditions and religious festivals.

Ethnic Groups

Ethiopia is ethnically diverse (Oromo, Amhara, Tigray, Somali, Sidama, Gurage, Afar, and many others).
