About Ethiopia

Ethiopia, officially the Federal Democratic Republic of Ethiopia, is a landlocked country in the Horn of Africa. It shares borders with Eritrea to the north, Djibouti to the northeast, Somalia to the east, Kenya to the south, South Sudan to the west, and Sudan to the northwest. With over 117 million inhabitants as of 2021, Ethiopia is the 12th most populous country in the world and the 2nd most populous in Africa.

Ethiopian History

Ethiopia is one of the oldest countries in the world and the only African country that was never colonized (except for a brief Italian occupation from 1936-1941).

Ethiopia's history dates back to the ancient Kingdom of D'mt (c. 980-400 BCE) and the Aksumite Empire (c. 100-940 CE), which was a major trading nation and one of the first to adopt Christianity as a state religion in the early 4th century.

The Zagwe dynasty ruled from the 10th to 13th centuries, famous for constructing rock-hewn churches in Lalibela. The Solomonic dynasty then claimed descent from King Solomon and the Queen of Sheba, ruling until 1974.

Emperor Menelik II defeated Italian forces at the Battle of Adwa in 1896, preserving Ethiopia's independence. Emperor Haile Selassie modernized the country but was overthrown in 1974, leading to a communist Derg regime. The current federal republic was established in 1991.

Geography & Regions

Ethiopia has a diverse topography that includes one of the world's largest uninterrupted mountain ranges, the Ethiopian Highlands, also known as the "Roof of Africa".

Highlands

The Ethiopian Highlands cover most of the country and have an average elevation of 2,000-3,000 meters, with the highest peak being Ras Dashen at 4,550 meters.

Great Rift Valley

The valley runs through Ethiopia from northeast to southwest, containing several lakes including Lake Tana, the source of the Blue Nile.

Climate Zones

Ethiopia has three main climate zones: the cool highlands (Dega), temperate midlands (Weyna Dega), and hot lowlands (Qolla).

Culture & Religion

Ethiopia has a rich cultural heritage with diverse traditions influenced by its many ethnic groups.

Religion

Ethiopia is predominantly Christian (Ethiopian Orthodox 43.8%, Protestant 22.8%) and Muslim (31.3%), with traditional beliefs and other religions making up the remainder.

Ethnic Groups

The Oromo (34.9%), Amhara (27.9%), and Somali (6.2%) are the largest ethnic groups, with over 80 distinct ethnicities in total.

Language

Amharic is the official language, but over 80 languages are spoken. English is widely taught in schools and used in government and business.

Economy

Ethiopia has one of the fastest-growing economies in Africa, with agriculture as the backbone of the economy, employing over 80% of the population.

Agriculture

Coffee is Ethiopia's primary export, along with oilseeds, pulses, flowers, and khat. The country is the largest coffee producer in Africa.

Industry

Key industries include food processing, beverages, textiles, leather, chemicals, and construction materials. Manufacturing is growing rapidly.

Services

The service sector is expanding, particularly in telecommunications, banking, and tourism, which is drawn to Ethiopia's historical sites and natural beauty.
